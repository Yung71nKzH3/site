Introduction

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) and robotics is fundamentally altering the entire human experience, ushering in an era of unprecedented technological integration. From self-driving cars navigating our streets to brain chips and robots making the impossible possible, AI is increasingly being interwoven into the fabric of our daily lives. This rapid evolution raises critical questions about the evolving relationship between humans and machines and the future of human-robot interaction.

This literature review investigates the dynamic nature of public opinion on human-robot interaction, exploring how these perceptions are likely to shift in the future. The review will examine key factors influencing this evolution, including:

  • Technological Advancements: Analyzing the impact of cutting-edge developments such as Elon Musk's Neuralink brain-computer interface, advancements in AI and machine learning, and the increasing sophistication of robotics, as exemplified by the work of Boston Dynamics, on public perceptions.

  • Public Opinion on Specific Applications: Investigating public attitudes towards AI and robotics in various domains, including government standings, healthcare, and the workplace, exploring concerns about job displacement and the potential for AI to augment or replace human capabilities.

  • Factors Influencing Public Opinion: Examining the multifaceted influences on public opinion, including media portrayals, cultural values, ethical considerations, and the role of trust and safety in the human-robot relationship.

  • Ethical Considerations and Public Debate: Analyzing the ethical dilemmas surrounding AI and robotics, including issues of privacy, autonomy, bias, and the responsible development and deployment of AI.

  • Future Trends and Predictions: Exploring potential future scenarios for human-robot interaction, including the concept of "cybernathrope" – the convergence of human and machine – and its implications for society.

By examining existing research and analyzing current trends, this review aims to provide insights into the potential trajectories of public opinion and their implications for the future of human-robot coexistence.
